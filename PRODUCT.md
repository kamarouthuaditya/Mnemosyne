# Mnemosyne

**Register:** product (data-review tool) with a brand-flavored editorial hero on the home surface.

## Product purpose

Mnemosyne is a personal career memory. It captures not just *what* Aditya Kamarouthu
builds, but *how he reasons*: every work entry carries context, options considered,
the decision, its rationale, the foresight at the time, and the eventual outcome. The
reasoning trail is the product. Most portfolios show shipped features; Mnemosyne shows
engineering judgment over time.

Data is self-assembled from real signals (git commits, changed files, PR descriptions,
Claude Code transcripts, TODOs) plus interview-captured PM reasoning, synced markdown →
Supabase → this Next.js app.

## Users

- **Aditya** reviewing his own decision history, building resume bullets, pre-interview prep.
- **Recruiters / hiring managers** assessing depth of judgment, not just a feature list.
- **Aditya's future self** recovering the *why* behind past calls.

## Creator

Aditya Kamarouthu, sole creator. Credit appears on the home surface and in the footer.

## Brand & tone

Editorial, literary, exact. Mnemosyne is the Greek goddess of memory; the app reads like
a printed monograph of one engineer's thinking. Calm, confident, no hype. Every word earns
its place.

## Strategic principles

1. **Reasoning over output.** Surface decision → rationale → outcome, not just titles.
2. **Editorial restraint.** Whitespace and typography carry the design; ornament does not.
3. **Legible at a glance, deep on demand.** Timeline and dashboard scan fast; entries drill deep.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark dev-tool dashboards (Linear/Vercel clones, neon-on-black).
- Hero-metric SaaS templates (big number + gradient accent).
- Card-grid soup where every item is an identical rounded box.
